## Risk table

| Risk ID | Description | Likelihood | Impact | Affected accounts / controls | Mitigation | Rollback |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **R-001** | **Enrolling the management account.** Landing the CT landing zone on the live `management` account (`000000000000`) mutates the org root, enables CT/StackSets trusted access, and lets CT manage the management context. A bad landing-zone apply can disrupt org-wide control plane (SCPs, Identity Center, billing). | Medium | Critical | `management` account; `aws_organizations_organization.this`; `aws_service_access_principals`; all root-attached SCPs | Take a full pre-enrollment snapshot (org tree, SCP attachments, Identity Center assignments, delegated admins). Verify break-glass IAM user (ADR-0011) login works. Enable CT trusted-access principals in a separate reviewed change first. Enroll during a change window with SOC on standby. | CT landing-zone setup is **largely irreversible** (see R-007). Rollback is limited to *decommissioning* the landing zone, which leaves CT-created shared accounts behind. Treat as forward-fix-only; keep break-glass IAM as the recovery path. |
| **R-002** | **Enrolling the prod account / Prod OU.** `prod` (`333333333333`) and `dr` (`666666666666`) sit in `Workloads/Prod`, which is at **5/5 SCPs**. Enrolling triggers CT guardrail attach → fails (see R-003) or applies new denies that can block live prod workloads (region, root, API shapes). | Medium | Critical | `prod`, `dr`; `Workloads/Prod` OU; `DenyRootAccount`, `RestrictRegions`, `DenyGuardDutyChanges`, `DenyDisableCloudTrail` | Free SCP slots on Prod **before** enrollment (R-003 mitigation). Enroll `dev`/`staging` first to validate the path. Diff CT guardrail effect against current SCPs to confirm no new deny breaks prod. Stage prod enrollment last, in a dedicated window. | Detach CT guardrails from the Prod OU and un-enroll the OU; re-attach the original hand-rolled SCPs from `terraform/modules/scps` via `terragrunt apply`. SCP changes are reversible; account enrollment itself is not (R-007). |
| **R-003** | **SCP slot exhaustion → guardrail-apply failure.** Workload OUs (`NonProd`, `Prod`) are at the AWS hard cap of **5 SCPs per target (incl. inherited FullAWSAccess)**. CT attaching even one managed guardrail fails the attach and **fails/partials the OU enrollment**. | High | High | `NonProd`, `Prod` OUs; all 5 attached SCPs each; root at 4/5 | **Pre-clean**: retire the SCPs CT's managed guardrails replace (`DenyDisableCloudTrail`, `DenyGuardDutyChanges`, region, `DenyRootAccount`) so each workload OU drops to ≤3/5. Use ADR-0017 relief (RCPs have a separate slot budget; EC2 Declarative Policies retire `require_imdsv2`). Keep only the non-duplicated SCPs (`DataPerimeter-DenyExternalPrincipals`, `DenyLeaveOrg`, `DenyAllSuspended`). | Re-attach the retired SCPs (revert the slot-clearing PR) — fully reversible via `for_each`/`count` in `terraform/modules/scps/main.tf` per `docs/scps.md` detach-then-delete procedure. |
| **R-004** | **Identity Center takeover locks out admins.** CT manages Identity Center; it can override/remove the 19 Terraform-managed assignments and the 6 permission sets in `terraform/modules/sso`. Group lookups by `DisplayName` or assignment removal can drop human SSO access. Region mismatch forces a brand-new instance. | Medium | Critical | All accounts; `aws_ssoadmin_permission_set.*`, `aws_ssoadmin_account_assignment.*`; groups `PlatformAdmins`/`Developers`/etc. | **Verify break-glass IAM login works immediately before** the Identity Center step. Snapshot all permission sets + assignments. Confirm CT **adopts the existing instance** (no second instance) and home-region matches. Split ownership: CT owns shared-account admin/readonly sets, Terraform keeps `PlatformEngineer`/`DeveloperAccess`/`SecurityAuditAccess`/`BillingAccess`. | Recover via the break-glass IAM user (ADR-0011, `prevent_destroy`, MFA). Re-apply the `sso` Terraform module to recreate Terraform-owned permission sets/assignments. Identity Center instance **adoption is not cleanly reversible** — do not let CT create a new instance. |
| **R-005** | **Delegated-admin reassignment breaks GuardDuty / Security Hub / Config.** CT moves delegated admin from the **management account** (current target for all three) to the **Audit account**. AWS allows one admin per service → reassignment de-registers management, can drop the org detector/aggregator, and `DenyGuardDutyChanges` (no exemptions) may block CT's re-association. | High | High | `guardduty-org`, `security-hub`/`securityhub-org`, `aws-config`/`config-org` delegated-admin settings; `DenyGuardDutyChanges` SCP; all member accounts' telemetry | Provision the Audit account first. Add CT trusted-access principals. **Temporarily relax `DenyGuardDutyChanges`** for the CT/service-linked principal during handover, re-tighten after. Update Terraform `delegated_admin_account_id` to the Audit account (or hand ownership to CT and remove from Terraform). Schedule a known telemetry-handover window; alert SOC. | Re-set `delegated_admin_account_id` back to the management account in the `guardduty-org`/`security-hub`/`aws-config` terragrunt units and `terragrunt apply`. Re-tighten `DenyGuardDutyChanges`. Reversible, but expect a telemetry gap during re-handover. |
| **R-006** | **Drift between Terraform state and CT-managed resources.** CT manages OUs/SCPs/accounts/Identity-Center/logging via its own control plane + StackSets, not Terraform. The `organization`, `scps`, `sso`, `guardduty-org`, `security-hub`, `aws-config`, `cloudtrail` modules will perpetually drift (CT-created Log Archive/Audit accounts not in `member_accounts`; CT SCPs/guardrails not in state; CT trusted-access principals added to the org resource). | High | Medium | `terraform/modules/organization` (`member_accounts`, `aws_service_access_principals`); `scps`; `sso`; security-service modules; CI plan/apply | Per primitive, decide ownership **before** enrollment: either stop managing it in Terraform (`ignore_changes`, remove resource, or `terraform state rm`) or import/reconcile it to CT's reality. Add CT-created shared accounts to `member_accounts` or exclude them from the org module's account management. Add CT/StackSets principals to `aws_service_access_principals` so the org resource stops drifting. | No destructive rollback needed — drift is reconciled by editing Terraform to match CT (the intended end state). Revertible by re-asserting Terraform ownership, but that re-introduces the fight; not recommended. |
| **R-007** | **Irreversibility of certain CT enrollment steps.** Landing-zone setup, shared-account creation (Log Archive, Audit), Identity Center instance adoption, and `feature_set=ALL` org changes are **one-way doors**. Decommissioning CT leaves orphaned shared accounts and StackSet instances; you cannot cleanly return to the exact hand-rolled state. | High | High | `management` account; CT-created Log Archive + Audit accounts; Identity Center instance; org `feature_set` | Treat enrollment as **forward-only**. Gate each irreversible step behind explicit human approval (per `.claude/rules/critical-decisions.md`). Validate the full path on `dev`/`staging` first. Snapshot everything restorable before each one-way step. Keep break-glass IAM as the independent recovery path. | **No true rollback** for the one-way steps. Mitigation is prevention: dry-run, snapshot, staged approval. Forward-fix any failure rather than attempting to un-enroll. |
| **R-008** | **CloudTrail / Config disruption and audit-trail gap.** Retiring the hand-rolled `org-trail` (in `management`) is blocked by `DenyDisableCloudTrail` (no exemptions). Object-Lock (365d) on the trail bucket prevents deletion. Config recorder collision (one per account/region) can fail CT account enrollment. The ADR-0011 break-glass alarm reads from the `org-trail` log group and goes silent if the trail moves. | Medium | High | `cloudtrail-org` (`org-trail`, bucket `cloudtrail-audit-logs-000000000000`); `DenyDisableCloudTrail` SCP; `aws-config` recorders; break-glass usage alarm | **Run CT's Log Archive trail in parallel** with `org-trail`; only retire `org-trail` after CT's pipeline is verified delivering. Relax `DenyDisableCloudTrail` solely for the retirement change (reviewed, audited). **Retain the Object-Locked bucket read-only** for its full retention. **Re-point the break-glass alarm** to CT's trail log group in the same PR. Pre-import/pre-delete colliding Config recorders before enrolling each account. | Re-enable the `org-trail` unit and re-tighten `DenyDisableCloudTrail` — reversible while the bucket still exists. The Object-Locked legacy logs are retained regardless, so the audit trail is never actually lost; the risk is a *monitoring/alerting* gap, not data loss. |
| **R-009** | **`DenyAllSuspended` / `OrganizationAccountAccessRole` carve-out lost.** CT could attempt to govern or re-baseline the `Suspended` OU (and `Policy-Staging`), overwriting the bespoke `DenyAllSuspended` SCP whose OAAR carve-out is load-bearing for break-glass and offboarding. | Low | High | `Suspended` OU; `Policy-Staging` OU; `DenyAllSuspended` SCP; `OrganizationAccountAccessRole` | **Do not enroll `Suspended` or `Policy-Staging`** under CT. Document them as Terraform-owned, un-governed exceptions. Verify post-enrollment that `DenyAllSuspended` and its OAAR carve-out are still attached unchanged. | Re-attach `DenyAllSuspended` to the `Suspended` OU via `terraform/modules/scps` (`suspended_ou_id` set) and `terragrunt apply`. Fully reversible. |
| **R-010** | **AFT pipeline / `Deployments` OU misconfiguration.** AFT (ADR-0017) introduces a CodePipeline/CodeBuild/Step-Functions vending pipeline driven by four `aft-*` repos, landing in the `Deployments` OU. Over-broad AFT IAM or a mis-scoped account-customization can vend accounts with weakened guardrails or punch through the data perimeter. | Medium | High | `Deployments` OU; AFT IAM principals; `aft-account-request`/`aft-*-customizations` repos; newly-vended accounts | Scope AFT pipeline IAM to least-privilege; `Deployments` OU SCPs deny workload data-plane access (per ADR-0001, excluded from `workload_ou_names` deliberately). Gate AFT policy changes with the ADR-0017 Access-Analyzer `CheckNoNewAccess` CI check (read JSON `result`, not exit code). Vend a throwaway test account first. | Disable the AFT pipeline; quarantine any mis-vended account by moving it to the `Suspended` OU (`DenyAllSuspended`). Revert the offending `aft-*` repo change. |

## Top-priority risks (do these first)

1. **R-003 (SCP slot exhaustion)** — High likelihood, hard-stops enrollment of
   workload OUs. **Pre-clean SCP slots before CT touches `NonProd`/`Prod`.**
2. **R-005 (delegated-admin reassignment)** — High likelihood, breaks live
   security telemetry. **Provision Audit account + sequence the handover; relax
   `DenyGuardDutyChanges` only during it.**
3. **R-007 / R-001 (irreversibility of management/landing-zone steps)** — Critical
   impact, one-way doors. **Snapshot + staged human approval + break-glass
   verified before each.**
4. **R-004 (Identity Center lock-out)** — Critical impact. **Verify break-glass
   IAM login works immediately before the Identity Center step.**

## Standing controls referenced

- **Break-glass IAM user** (ADR-0011) — the independent recovery path for R-001,
  R-004, R-007. Must be verified working before each irreversible/SSO step.
- **`DenyAllSuspended` + Suspended OU** — the quarantine path used as rollback for
  R-010 and protected by R-009.
- **ADR-0017 relief levers** — RCPs (separate slot budget) and EC2 Declarative
  Policies (retire `require_imdsv2`) are the structural fix for R-003 slot
  pressure and should land before workload-OU enrollment.
